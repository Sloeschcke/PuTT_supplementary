Incomplete Data

Visual comparison of PuTT against baseline methods under three scenarios of training on incomplete data related to the resuls in Figure 11 of the paper. We use images with a 4k resolution and each model has a compression factor of 30. The first three images display training data with 99%, 95%, and 90% missing data, respectively. Use the buttons below to view results for each scenario, comparing PuTT and baseline methods with and without upsampling. The top row showcases results with upsampling, while the bottom row presents outcomes without upsampling. For a more detailed examination, click on the images for a full-screen view.






Noisy Data

Visual comparison of PuTT against baseline methods in the context of noisy data samples. We use images with a 4k resolution and each model has a compression factor of 30. This analysis corresponds to Figure 12 in the paper and focuses on three different levels of Gaussian noise with (σ) values of 0.1, 0.5, and 1.0. By clicking on the buttons provided, you can explore the impact of noise on each method's performance. For a closer look at the details, click on the images to enlarge them to full-screen view.




Number of Upsampling Steps - Noisy Data

Here we present a side-by-side visual comparison of TT No Upsampling and our PuTT with 7 upsampling steps.
